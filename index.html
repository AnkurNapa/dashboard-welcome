About the ferment
≈

Sugar → alcohol + CO₂

Yeast eats the grape sugar, giving off alcohol and the carbon-dioxide bubbles rising through every tank.

°Bx

Brix falls to dry

Must starts near 22–25 °Brix and drops toward 0 as the sugar is consumed — that's how you track progress.

🌡

Temperature steers style

Reds ferment warm (24–30 °C) to pull colour and tannin; whites stay cool (12–18 °C) to protect aromatics.

⏳

5–14 days of primary

Primary fermentation runs about one to two weeks before the wine is pressed off and moved to barrel or tank.
